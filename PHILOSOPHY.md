## What Ukiyo-Tone Is

**Ukiyo-Tone is a personal visual instrument for thinking in code.**

It is not a theme optimized for novelty, screenshots, or mass customization.  
It is a deliberately constrained environment designed to *disappear* once deep focus begins.

The goal is not excitement.  
The goal is **clarity with character**.

---

## Core Philosophy

### Restraint Over Abundance
Ukiyo-Tone rejects excess.

- No unnecessary colors  
- No ornamental contrast  
- No features added without structural purpose  

Every visual choice must justify itself through readability, hierarchy, or focus.

---

### Structure Before Decoration
Color is treated as **syntax**, not paint.

- Keywords define grammar  
- Functions express motion  
- Variables carry weight  
- Comments annotate rather than compete  

Nothing exists purely for aesthetics.

---

### Organic, Not Mechanical
Inspired by Ukiyo-e woodblock prints and Sumi-e ink traditions:

- Slight softness is intentional  
- Perfect neutrality is avoided  
- Visual breathing room is respected  

The interface should feel *worked by hand*, not machined.

---

### Long-Session Ergonomics
Ukiyo-Tone is designed for endurance.

- Hours, not minutes  
- Reading, not scanning  
- Debugging, not demos  

If a choice improves screenshots but harms long-term focus, it is rejected.
